University Writing Requirement

College Writing Program

The College Writing Program acknowledges the considerable existing strengths of writers coming to Washington University and is designed to extend those strengths, inviting students to become more thoughtful, efficient, and nuanced writers of academic and professional prose. In its largest sense, the course College Writing 1 (L59 100) is about how writers use language to generate original ideas and how they deploy those ideas to influence specific audiences.

Preparatory Courses for College Writing 1

In certain instances, students may be required to complete preparatory coursework prior to taking College Writing 1 and may be placed into either 1511 or 1001. Students placed in one of the preparatory courses must successfully complete that course (by earning a C- or better) and the course that follows (if applicable) in order, in successive semesters before they may take College Writing 1. Only satisfactory performance in the course (earning a C- or better) will fulfill the first year writing requirement.

AP/IB Test Scores

Policy for Architecture, Art, Arts & Sciences, and Business

Test scores cannot exempt students from taking L59-100. Students entering the university with an AP score of 5 or an IB score of 7, and who earn a B or better in 100, satisfy the writing requirement as well as receive 3 elective back-credits, which count toward graduation. Students who have a 5 on the AP test or a 7 on the IB test, and who receive a B- or below in L59-100, satisfy the writing requirement but do not receive additional elective credits. Students are encouraged to verify that their AP or IB score has been communicated to the university and that it appears on their record. Contact the Office of Student Records with any questions.

Policy for Engineering Students

Students in Engineering should consult School of Engineering and Applied Sciences Writing Requirement.
